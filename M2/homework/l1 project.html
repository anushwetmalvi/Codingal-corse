Facts about prominent space agencies

NASA, ISRO, and JAXA are prominent space agencies, each with distinct strengths and missions. NASA, the US space agency, focuses on deep space exploration, scientific research, and technological development. ISRO, India's space agency, emphasizes cost-effective missions, technological advancements, and applications of space technology for societal benefit. JAXA, Japan's space agency, focuses on research, technology development, and satellite launches, with a strong emphasis on planetary exploration.

NASA (National Aeronautics and Space Administration): Key Achievements: Apollo missions (moon landing), Hubble Space Telescope, Mars rovers, and the International Space Station. Recent Projects: Artemis program (returning humans to the Moon), James Webb Space Telescope, and missions to Mars and other planets. Budget: NASA's fiscal year 2024 budget is $24.875 billion.

ISRO (Indian Space Research Organisation): Key Achievements: Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan), Chandrayaan missions (Moon exploration), and PSLV (Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle). Recent Projects: Gaganyaan (crewed space mission), Aditya-L1 (solar observation), and Chandrayaan-3 (lunar exploration). Budget: ISRO's budget for 2024 is approximately $2.5 billion.

JAXA (Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency): Key Achievements: Hayabusa asteroid missions, Kibo module on the ISS, and Akasaki satellite for Earth observation. Recent Projects: Martian Moons eXploration (MMX), SLIM (Smart Lander for Investigating Moon), and lunar exploration missions. Budget: JAXA's budget for 2024 is approximately $2 billion.
